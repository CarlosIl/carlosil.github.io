Landmarks


Iglesia de San Lorenzo


The Church of San Lorenzo, built entirely of brick, is the other jewel of the Romanesque style of Sahagún. Perfect geometry of Moorish brick, it has the same architectural characteristics as that of San Tirso, floor plan, naves and apses, as well as a magnificent four-story tower that decreases in height.

Iglesia de la Peregrina


It was a Franciscan convent founded around 1257, today the Sanctuary of the Pilgrim is located on the outskirts of the town, sitting on a hill. 

Arco de San Benito


It was built in 1662 according to the design of the architect Felipe Berrojo, to replace the previous Romanesque doorway that was in ruins. The N-120 as it passes through Sahagún was designed to pass under the arch as a triumphal arch. Among the architectural elements that stand out the most are the central shield, the inscriptions and the statues that complete the whole, the two statues that are in the two niches with the images of the saints Facundo and Primitivo.

Ermita de la Virgen del Puente


La ermita se encuentra a unos tres kilómetros al este de Sahagún. Es la primera edificación que encontramos en el Camino francés (Camino de Santiago) al entrar en tierras de León.
Al estar junto al puente sobre el río Valderaduey fue lo que le dio el nombre con que se conoce. Su arquitectura responde al estilo mudejar, posee una sola nave y una cabecera muy pequeña con un ábside en semihexágono, arquerías ciegas y frisos de esquina.
