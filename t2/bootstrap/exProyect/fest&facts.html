Situada al sureste de la provincia de León, en la zona noroccidental de la comunidad autónoma de Castilla y León. Ubicada entre los ríos Cea y Valderaduey, se encuentra en la comarca Tierra de Sahagún que comparte con los municipios limítrofes de Cea, Grajal de Campos o Calzada del Coto

The shape of the field is traditional Spanish, rectangular, quadrilateral and rounded at the bottom -carpanel arch upside down-. Its proportion is five wide by six high, and it is acaudado since it is not finished off with a point. As for the partitions of this field, it appears with a "single quarter with bordure".

On a field of gold, an armed horseman (Saint Martin, bishop of Tours), with sword raised in the right hand on a horse passing to the left on a sinople terracotta, who gives his cape of gules to a man in full body passing in the same direction, half dressed with a skirt of azure and a crutch to support a limp.

Alternating four lions of purple on a silver field and on gules other four castles donjonados of gold. It must be stamped with a closed royal crown.

The coat of arms of the town of Sahagún has a historical character that is given by seeing its arms represented throughout the last centuries.

Regarding the antiquity of the use of this coat of arms by the municipal institution of this town, it is worth considering its possible use prior to the 18th century, as demonstrated by an engraving dated 1745, dedicated to San Juan de Sahagún in the collection "Tabularium Artis Asturicensis" in Oviedo. And in the sixth volume of the work "Nobiliario de los Reinos y Señoríos de España..." authored by Francisco Piferrer (1860), where the coat of arms of Sahagún also appears.
